SMSGGDJ FM preset patcher

Drop a built .sms ROM to edit its 8 custom FM presets (the YM2413 user patches selected by an FM instrument's PRST field). Each preset is a 2-operator voice — a modulator feeding a carrier — plus feedback and a name. Download the patched ROM and load it in the tracker (or an emulator) to hear it; there's no FM audio in this page. Nothing leaves your browser.

what the parameters mean

Each preset is a two-operator FM voice: the carrier is the operator you actually hear, and the modulator bends the carrier's waveform to create the timbre. The modulator's TL sets how strong that effect is.

MUL
frequency multiplier — the operator's pitch as a multiple of the note (1×, 2×…); mismatched mod/carrier ratios give richer/clangier tones
AR
attack rate — how fast the note reaches full level (15 = instant, 0 = never)
DR
decay rate — how fast it falls from the peak down to the sustain level
SL
sustain level — the level it holds at after decay (0 = loudest, 15 = quietest)
RR
release rate — how fast it fades once the note ends
TL
total level (modulator only) — modulation depth / brightness; lower = brighter, higher = closer to a pure sine
KSL
key-scale level — how much higher notes are attenuated
FB
feedback — the modulator feeding back into itself; adds harmonics/grit (0–7)

Switches: AM tremolo · VIB vibrato · SUS sustained envelope (note holds at SL instead of decaying away) · KSR key-scale rate (higher notes decay faster) · HALF half-sine waveform for that operator (brighter, buzzier).

The ▶ button on each preset auditions it with a built-in 2-operator FM synth. It models the YM2413's envelopes, feedback, multipliers and waveforms closely enough to hear what each control does, but it is an approximation — the real chip is the final word.
